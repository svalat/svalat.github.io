    Current position

    I am currently a fellow at CERN working on the R&D of the future data acquisition system (DAQ) of LHCb in collaboration with Intel. The challenge of this project is making a DAQ without any hardware triggers which will be a first time for such a large detector.

    Removing the first level hardware trigger will imply the capability of handling 40 Tb/s in the DAQ. In order to manage this high throughput we currently study high-speed fabrics coming from the HPC field : Intel Omni-Path and Mellanox InfiniBand. Both run at 100 Gb/s.

    The final system will need to scale on 500 nodes to fulfill the required bandwidth considering 80 Gb/s per node. After being aggregated on those 500 nodes, the data will need to be dispatched on 4000 nodes for triggering decision before reaching long term storage for physics analysis.

    PhD.
